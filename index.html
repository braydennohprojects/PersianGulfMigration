Persian Gulf Settlement Simulator

Settlements cluster near water for economic benefit and group together to share infrastructure, but over-concentration is penalized by a crowding term that produces a natural optimal cluster size. Agents resist moving unless the pressure to relocate becomes strong enough. When the sea level changes, the coastline shifts and agents on newly flooded cells are displaced.

The attractor can be set to rivers only (agents seek proximity to river channels) or river + ocean (any water body attracts). When the sea level rises and submerges a river cell, that cell becomes ocean.

The grid has three cell types: ocean (elevation ≤ sea level), river (Strahler order ≥ threshold, not submerged), and land. Settlements \(V(\mathbf{x},t)=1\) are shown in red. Population is conserved: \(\sum V = N\).

Each land cell has a utility

\[ U(\mathbf{x}) \;=\; -\,w_d \, \mathcal{D}(\mathbf{x}) \;+\; w_c \, \mathcal{C}(\mathbf{x}) \;-\; w_r \, \mathcal{C}(\mathbf{x})^2 \]

where \(\mathcal{D}\) is the Euclidean distance to the nearest attractor water cell and \(\mathcal{C}\) counts occupied Moore neighbors. The first two terms drive agents toward water and toward each other; the quadratic crowding term \(w_r\) penalizes over-concentration, producing an optimal cluster size at \(\mathcal{C}^* = w_c / 2w_r\). The attractor set is controlled by the toggle below: either rivers only, or rivers + ocean. The friction threshold \(\mu\) governs voluntary migration: the worst cell swaps with the best empty cell only if the gain exceeds \(\mu\).
